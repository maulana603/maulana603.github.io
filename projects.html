Research projects



Rational peptide design for regulating liquid–liquid phase separation

Many proteins were found to induce liquid-liquid phase separation (LLPS) in living cells via multivalent interactions between the intrinsically disordered regions (IDRs). Regulation of LLPS by additives is challenging but is expected to have useful applications. Our group established a rational design method of peptides binding to IDRs and succeeded in regulating the DNA-binding ability of a tumor suppressor p53 by the peptides. In this research, I made a program in Perl to predict the best sequence for targeting N-terminal of p53. The result is our designed peptide enhanced the LLPS property of p53. I also succesfully reduced the LLPS property of p53 by attaching MBP protein to desgined peptide.

Synthetic peptide for surface of globular protein

This project is continuation of my bachelor's thesis. 3D structures of proteins are different from their respective long chain of amino acids. There are some buried amino acids and surface amino acids. If we can make a peptide that targets the surface of amino acids, we would have interaction between them. The application from this work is limitless. It could be applied to make an inhibitor that binds to the active site of enzymes, which can be used as therapeutics and drugs. I made the program in Python using BioPython to extract the structure of protein from PDB files and to predict the best sequence for binding peptide. The project is continued by my junior and is not finished yet.
